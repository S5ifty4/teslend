# Teslend Marketing Playbook

## Strategy: Supply First
Need listers before renters. Target people who already own the gear.

## Channel Outreach Copy (ready to use)

### 1. Facebook Marketplace DM
Target: People listing Tesla accessories for sale (roof racks, hitch racks, bike carriers)
Angle: "Rent instead of sell — earn $20-40/day, keep the item"

> Hey — saw your listing for the [item]. Before you sell it, wanted to share something that might be worth considering.
>
> I run a small platform called Teslend (teslend.com) — basically a rental marketplace specifically for Tesla accessories in the Bay Area. Instead of a one-time sale, you could list it for rent and earn $20–40/day whenever someone needs it for a weekend trip, ski season, camping, etc. You keep the item and keep making money from it.
>
> No fees to list, no commitment. If it doesn't work out you can always sell it later.
>
> Totally up to you — just thought it was worth mentioning before you let it go for a fraction of what it could earn over time. Happy to answer any questions if you're curious.

---

### 2. Reddit Launch Post
Target: r/TeslaModelY, r/TeslaMotorsModel3, r/bayarea
Title: "I built a rental marketplace for Tesla accessories — Bay Area folks, would love your feedback"

> Hey — Bay Area Tesla owner here. I kept running into the same problem: I'd want a roof rack or bike carrier for a weekend trip, but didn't want to spend $400 on something I'd use twice a year. Meanwhile I knew other Tesla owners had that exact gear sitting in their garage.
>
> So I built Teslend (teslend.com) — a peer-to-peer rental marketplace specifically for Tesla accessories. Roof racks, hitch racks, bike carriers, cargo boxes, camping gear. You list what you own, set your daily price, and renters reach out directly. No middleman, no app, no payments through us — you arrange everything between yourselves.
>
> It's free to list, free to browse. Bay Area first while we figure out if this is useful to people.
>
> If you've got gear collecting dust or you've ever wished you could rent a roof rack for a Tahoe weekend without buying one — check it out. Honest feedback welcome, even if it's "this is a terrible idea."

---

### 3. Tesla Owners Silicon Valley Facebook Group
> For Bay Area Tesla owners — I built something you might find useful.
>
> It's called Teslend (teslend.com). Simple idea: rent Tesla accessories from other local owners instead of buying gear you'll only use a few times a year. Roof racks, bike carriers, hitch racks, camping gear — listed by Tesla owners for Tesla owners.
>
> If you've got accessories sitting unused: free to list, you set your own price, renters contact you directly. No platform fees, no middleman.
>
> Bay Area only for now. Happy to answer questions.

---

### 4. Tesla Motors Club Forum
Target: Marketplace or Bay Area regional section
Title: "Teslend — P2P Tesla accessory rentals, Bay Area (free to list)"

> Hey TMC — Bay Area member here. Built a small platform for renting Tesla accessories peer-to-peer: teslend.com
>
> If you own a roof rack, hitch rack, bike carrier, or camping gear that sits unused most of the year, you can list it and earn rental income when other Tesla owners need it. Renters get access to expensive accessories for a weekend without buying them outright.
>

>
> Currently focused on San Francisco Bay Area.
